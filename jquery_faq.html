Hoodoos (irregular columns of rock) exist on every continent, but here is the largest concentration found anywhere on Earth.

Grand Canyon NP
Unique combinations of geologic color and erosional forms decorate a canyon that is 277 river miles (446km) long, up to 18 miles (29km) wide, and a mile (1.6km) deep.
Devil's Tower NP
Devil's Tower features heavily in several films including Close Encounters of the Third Kind.
Yosemite NP
First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more.
Olympic NP
Encompassing nearly a million acres, the park protects a vast wilderness, thousands of years of human history, and several distinctly different ecosystems, including glacier-capped mountains, old-growth temperate rain forests, and over 70 miles of wild coastline.
